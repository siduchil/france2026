🚗 Driving Guide — First Time in France

⚠️ #1 Rule: DRIVE ON THE RIGHT!

You're used to left-hand driving in the UK. The most dangerous moments are roundabouts, turning out of petrol stations, and early mornings. Your wife should remind you at every junction for the first day.

🛣️ Speed Limits

  • Motorway (Autoroute): 130 km/h (110 in rain)
  • Dual carriageway: 110 km/h
  • Rural roads: 80 km/h
  • Towns: 50 km/h (30 in some zones)
  • Speed cameras are EVERYWHERE — use Waze app

💰 Motorway Tolls (Péage)

  • Budget ~€200-300 total for your route
  • Most accept contactless card payment
  • Consider a Bip&Go toll tag (order online) to skip queues
  • Alternative: Use 'N' roads (free but slower, more scenic)

⛽ Fuel Tips

  • Supermarket fuel stations (Carrefour, Leclerc) are cheapest
  • Motorway fuel is 20-30% more expensive
  • Fill up before getting on the autoroute
  • Budget ~€250-350 total for 3,000+ km

🅿️ Parking

  • Blue zones: Need a parking disc (disque de stationnement) — buy at tabac shops
  • Pay & display: Look for horodateur machines
  • Many cities have underground car parks (€10-20/day)
  • Free parking often available on outskirts + walk in

🚨 SOLO DRIVER — You're Doing All 3,000+ km Alone

This is manageable but you MUST respect your limits. No one else can take over. Plan your days around driving fatigue, not sightseeing ambition.

😴 Solo Driver Fatigue Plan

  • HARD RULE: Stop every 90 minutes, no exceptions. Get out, walk the dog, stretch
  • French motorway rest areas (Aires) are every 20-30 km — use them liberally
  • Your hardest days: Day 1 (5-6 hrs), Day 4 (3h10m), Day 13 (4h30m)
  • Leave EARLY on long drive days (6-7 AM) — you drive better fresh
  • No driving after dark if you can avoid it — unfamiliar roads + right-hand side + fatigue = danger
  • Wife's job: navigator, DJ, snack supplier, and keeping you talking/alert
  • Keep water, coffee, and glucose sweets within reach
  • Download 5-6 podcasts/audiobooks per long drive day
  • If you feel ANY drowsiness: pull over immediately, 20-min power nap, coffee, then wait 20 mins for caffeine to kick in
  • Don't push through — you have the dog and your wife in the car
  • Consider breaking Day 13 (Lyon→Dijon, 4h30m) with a lunch stop halfway

📋 Legal Requirements in the Car

  • ✅ Hi-vis vest x2 (inside car, NOT in boot)
  • ✅ Warning triangle
  • ✅ UK driving licence + IDP recommended
  • ✅ Crit'Air vignette on windscreen
  • ✅ Headlight beam deflectors (or auto-adjusting)
  • ✅ GB sticker if no GB on number plate
  • ✅ Rental agreement + insurance docs

🔄 Roundabouts

  • Traffic ON the roundabout has priority (opposite to some UK habits)
  • Enter from the RIGHT lane
  • Signal LEFT when entering, RIGHT when exiting
  • Priorité à droite: At unmarked junctions, give way to traffic from the RIGHT

⚠️ Important EV Charging Tips

  • Always have a backup station in mind — chargers can be out of service
  • Charge to 80% max on fast chargers (80-100% is very slow)
  • Drive at 110-120 km/h instead of 130 to gain 15-20% more range
  • Pre-condition battery before arriving at charger (most EVs do this via nav)
  • Carry the car's included charging cable — some Airbnbs have regular plugs you can trickle-charge from overnight
  • French motorway chargers accept contactless card, but an RFID card is faster

⚡ Electric Car Option — Full Analysis

Why Consider EV?

An electric car is automatically Crit'Air 0 — exempt from ALL French Low Emission Zones. No sticker needed, no fines, no stress. Drive freely through Paris, Nice, Lyon, Montpellier.

✅ Pros

  • Zero LEZ risk — drive anywhere freely
  • Fuel cost: ~£80-120 vs £250-350 petrol
  • Charging stops = forced rest breaks (good for solo driver!)
  • Quieter, smoother motorway driving = less fatigue
  • Regenerative braking on mountain/coastal roads
  • No Crit'Air sticker hassle at all
  • French motorway charging is good on your A6/A7/A8 route

⚠️ Cons

  • Rental costs ~£10-20/day more (~£150-300 extra total)
  • Charging adds 30-80 min on long drive days
  • Need to plan charging stops (download ABRP app)
  • Some Airbnbs may not have charging — need public chargers
  • Motorway speed (130 km/h) drains battery faster
  • Range anxiety on Day 1 (456 km) and Day 13 (487 km)
  • If charger is broken/busy, you wait
